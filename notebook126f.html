Notebook Setup

Unique Violations

  • Each robot is limited to two rings and one mobile goal
    • Violators must immediately remove field elements from their possession
    • Rings on stakes do not count toward the limit of rings
  • Robots are allowed to plow multiple goals at the same time
    • Not allowed to plow goals (generally) when a robot is already possessing a mobile goal
    • Examples of match-affecting possession violations
      • Possessing two or more Mobile Goals in a single Corner.
      • Continuing to play other portions of the game (e.g., defensive maneuvers, Climbing) without
      • returning to a starting position for the majority of the Match.
      • “Accidentally” Possessing an egregious amount of opponent Rings.
      • Possessing six Rings, and Scoring them onto one Stake with a single action.
      • “Removing excess Scoring Objects” directly into Placed/Scored states
  • Robots cannot cross the autonomous line
    • Result is an automatic forfeit of the autonomous bonus to the opposing alliance
  • Opponents cannot be removed from the ladder once they have climbed
    • Treated similarly to tipping violations
    • If robot has non-robust system, prone to falling off without opponent interaction, violations will not always be called
  • Alliance wall stakes are protected
    • May not interact at all

Initial Analysis

Goals

We want to determine what the focus should be for this year’s game.

Match Loading & Drive Team Roles

Most of the scoring objects (rings) start off on the field. While sometimes, more rings will be placed on the field if one goes out of bounds, the overall prevalence of match loading is significantly less than that of over under. The drive team’s role will be more as guides for the driver, giving advice and time callouts. Less time will have to be devoted toward practicing match loading and more time should be devoted toward practicing drive team communication.

Possessing Rings

This game will require robots to pick up rings that are flat on the ground and place them on top of a 14” pole. This means that the robot will need to lift up the rings off the ground and elevate it. This could be done with either two mechanisms or one.

Possessing Mobile Goals

Mobile goals can be possessed by robots and have the rings on the mobile goal not count toward the two ring limit. It would be extremely advantageous to be able to score rings on a mobile goal that you already possess. However, unlike Spinup, rings must be loaded from the top.

Physicality

The game perimeters seemed to be very physical in nature. In order to to do well in this competition, we would need to design a robot that would not be easily pushed around, had a secure mechanism for holding the ball, and had a high RPM drivetrain to push around other bots and moved well throughout the field.

Section Overview

Wheel Stack

Challenge Statement

We need to create a wheel stack that is small and has as thin a profile as possible, while also creating the most stable base possible. In addition, they should spin with as little friction as possible.

Problem Description

The wheel stack will determine the gear ratio for the bot as well as largely determining how many wheels the bot will have. This all affects how stable the bot is. In addition, the profile depth of the wheels will determine how much space there will be for the rest of the bot. Ideally, the wheels are as thin as possible, however, we need to balance our need for space with our need for stability, the wheels cannot be too thin otherwise the bot is easy to tip and the gears cannot be too thin otherwise they won’t mesh as well with the wheels and power will be lost in transfer.

Solution Requirements

  • Less than 2.5 inches wide
  • Use wheels shorter than three inches
  • Have gears on wheel similar to gears in motors (high gear reaction)
  • Be able to free spin for 15 seconds or more

Challenge Statement

We want to create a drivetrain that is heavy and stable. In order to achieve stable, we will need to create a center of mass that is low to the ground. In order to achieve heavy we will need to think about what materials we should use.

The main issues with our goal for the drivetrain is keeping it stable and with a low center of mass. This year, bots are required to place rings on high stakes meaning bots are required to be tall. If we want to have a bot with a low center of mass, our drivetrain need to compensate for this.
